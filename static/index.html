How RealText Detects AI Text

RealText uses algorithmic stylistic analysis — not a black-box AI model. It measures specific, interpretable characteristics of your writing:

Burstiness: Human writing naturally alternates between long and short sentences. AI tends to produce sentences of uniform length. RealText measures this variance.

Lexical Diversity (TTR): The ratio of unique words to total words. AI text often reuses the same vocabulary, while humans draw from a richer word pool.

Repetition Patterns: AI frequently repeats structural patterns ("Furthermore,... Furthermore,... Furthermore,..."). RealText detects these n-gram repetitions.

AI-Typical Connectors: Phrases like "It is important to note that," "In conclusion," or "Moreover" appear at unusually high rates in AI-generated text. RealText counts these per language.

These metrics are combined into a composite score. Then, an LLM generates human-readable suggestions in the language of your text, specifically targeting the weak areas identified by the metrics.
